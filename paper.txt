    ABSTRACT                                                                                                                               ARTICLE HISTORY
    Cross-validation is one of the most popular model and tuning parameter selection methods in statistics                                 Received April 2018
    and machine learning. Despite its wide applicability, traditional cross-validation methods tend to overfit,                            Accepted September 2019
    due to the ignorance of the uncertainty in the testing sample. We develop a novel statistically principled
    inference tool based on cross-validation that takes into account the uncertainty in the testing sample.                                KEYWORDS
                                                                                                                                           Cross-validation; Hypothesis
    This method outputs a set of highly competitive candidate models containing the optimal one with
                                                                                                                                           testing; Model selection;
    guaranteed probability. As a consequence, our method can achieve consistent variable selection in a                                    Overfitting; Tuning
    classical linear regression setting, for which existing cross-validation methods require unconventional split                          parameter selection
    ratios. When used for tuning parameter selection, the method can provide an alternative trade-off between
    prediction accuracy and model interpretability than existing variants of cross-validation. We demonstrate
    the performance of the proposed method in several simulated and real data examples. Supplemental
    materials for this article can be found online.
